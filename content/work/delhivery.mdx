## the problem

Delhivery’s routing engine systematically underestimates delivery times —
only 4.1% of legs land within ±15% of the OSRM estimate. And the delay is
heavily concentrated: the top 5 hubs account for 30.8% of all network
excess delay, led by Gurgaon Bilaspur HB alone at 12.5%.<FootnoteMark />

<Footnote>
  worst corridor: Gurgaon ⇄ Bangalore — ~145k excess minutes in three weeks.
</Footnote>

## what i built

Delhivery’s network, modelled as a directed weighted graph from ~144,900
scan events (~14,800 trips), with a graph-enhanced XGBoost ETA model on
top of it. Around the model: an FTL-vs-carting decision framework, a
7-script reproducible pipeline, a consulting-style strategy memo, and a
live 4-tab Streamlit operations dashboard.

## how it works

node2vec hub embeddings, betweenness and PageRank features, and corridor
history priors give the model a sense of where each leg sits in the
network — and leakage-safe train folds keep the evaluation honest.
